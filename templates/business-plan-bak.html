Mission & Vision Statement
This section explains the why the business exists. It expounds on the company's values and what does it aim to accomplish in the long run. The mission and vision statement should be concise and clear lines. It should be the ethos of the organisation and ingrained in the organisation.

Executive summary
The executive summary of a business is a brief introduction of the entire business plan. It provides a short, concise, and optimistic overview of business that captures the reader's attention. The executive summary is usually the first thing a reader will read and if it's poorly written, it will also be the last thing. The executive summary should be relatively short and succinct.

Company Description
A company description provides a basic and useful introduction. It provides the key deyails about a company. It gives out vital information such as where company is located, how large the company is, what is the business of the company and what company wishes to accomplish. It should give a brief intro about what products and services does the company offer.

Objectives
Objectives highlights the long term goals of the company in the form of financial, strategic and operational segment. Objectives should be SMART and realistic.
The SMART (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Realistic, and Timed) structure is useful for testing assumptions and ensuring that it can really be implemented.

Industry Analysis
In this section, you will provide the data and statistics regarding the industry and markets in which the company plans to enter. Analysing the information will enable you to identify the risks and opportunities confronting the company and develop strategies to take the full benefit of opportunities and devising the ways to minimise the impact of adverse situations.

Marketing Plan

The marketing plan section of the business plan explains how company is going to get their customers to buy its products and/or services. The plan should explain the characteristics of your market and suggest how much of the market company is expecting to capture. A strong Market plan also shows why customers want to buy what you offer rather that the competition’s products or services.

Ownership and Management Plan

The Management and Ownership section of a business plan features short (one to three paragraphs) biographies of the key personnel involved in forming and running the business. It covers the sections like management summary, personnel hiring plan, corporate structure and job descriptions etc.

Milestones

Milestones breakdown a plan into quantifiable dates with specific deadlines, and management responsibilities. Milestones lists all key actions and goals for products, services, marketing, administration, and finance. It includes launch dates, review dates, prototype availabilities, advertising, social media, website development, and programs to generate leads and traffic. It also includes goals in terms of reaching X sales by Y date etc.
